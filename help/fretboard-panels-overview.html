Fretboard panels overview

Frettly was designed not to simply show a single fretboard with a scale, which would reset next time the application was started, but as a macOS document based application capable of displaying many fretboards at a time, collected within each document.

  • Remember - all fretboards within the same document are for that documents current instrument/tuning/handedness configuration
  • When fretboards are added to the document, they are appended to the bottom of the document. Click and drag a fretboard, and drop it before or after any other fretboard to re-order your fretboards.
  • Zoom the fretboard's within a document with standard macOS ⌘+ and ⌘- key combos
  • If a fretboard's frets are off screen, scroll the fretboard with trackpad or mouse

Frettly fretboard panel features

  • To edit the type/root/pattern of a fret board, click the config icon to the right of the panels title to open its edit dialog.
  • The notes comprising each fretboard's scale, mode or chord note sequence are shown centre top. These show the note and its interval within the sequence from :

    P1 - Perfect unison (root)
    m2 - Minor second
    M2 - Major second
    m3 - Minor third
    M3 - Major third
    P4 - Perfect fourth
    d5 - Diminished fifth
    A4 - Augmented fourth
    P5 - Perfect fifth
    A5 - Augmented fifth
    m6 - Minor sixth
    M6 - Major sixth
    d7 - Major sixth
    m7 - Minor seventh
    M7 - Major seventh
    P8 - Perfect octave
    m9 - Minor ninth
    M9 - Major ninth
    m10 - Minor tenth
    M10 - Major tenth
    P11 - Perfect eleventh
    A11 - Augmented eleventh
    P12 - Perfect twelfth
    m13 - Minor thirteenth
    M13 - Major thirteenth

    Hovering over a chip highlights the note/interval's occurrences across the fretboard, while clicking it will toggle between hiding and showing it across the fretboard.
  • The icon to the left of that same pencil toggles the fretboards displaying of either notes or intervals
  • The coloured buttons centered above each fretboard serve several purposes
    • Display the colour coded note and its interval within the scale/mode/chord
    • Hover over one of those buttons and the occurrences on the fretboard are highlighted
    • Click one of those buttons, and that note/interval will be removed from the fretboard (you can click again to bring it back!)
