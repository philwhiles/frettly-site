Fretboard panels overview

Frettly was designed not to simply show a single fretboard with a scale, which would reset next time the application was started, but as a macOS document based application capable of displaying many fretboards at a time, collected within each document.

  • Remember - all fretboards within the same document are for that documents current instrument/tuning/handedness configuration
  • When fretboards are added to the document, they are appended to the bottom of the document. Click and drag a fretboard, and drop it before or after any other fretboard to re-order your fretboards.
  • Zoom the fretboards within a document with standard macOS ⌘+ and ⌘- key combos
  • If a fretboards frets are off screen, scroll the fretboard with trackpad or mouse
  • To edit the type/root/pattern of a fret board, click the pencil icon at the top right of the fretboard to open its edit dialog.
  • The edit dialog (pencil icon again!) also allows you to delete a fretboard from the document
  • The icon to the left of that same pencil toggles the fretboards displaying of either notes or intervals
  • The coloured buttons centered above each fretboard serve several purposes
    • Display the colour coded note and its interval within the scale/mode/chord
    • Hover over one of those buttons and the occurrences on the fretboard are highlighted
    • Click one of those buttons, and that note/interval will be removed from the fretboard (you can click again to bring it back!)
