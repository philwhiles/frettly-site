Explore your fretboard with context.

Frettly is a macOS application dedicated to enabling players of fretted instruments to visualise, experiment and explore scales, modes and chords in a document-like canvas.
Supporting Guitar, Bass Guitar, Mandolin, Banjo and Ukelele, each document you create can show multiple scales/chords/modes for the documents chosen instrument, and each instrument type supports multiple possible tunings.
Edit the instrument configuration of a document on the fly and instantly all of the panels displaying scales etc will reset to show the original scale, but on the newly selected instrument and/or tuning.

Build pages of panels, each showing a chord, scale, or pattern. Each document can store dozend of panels. Rearrange them by dragging, scroll horizontally to see the full fretboard or zoom in and out.

Scroll vertically through your document or use the 'Jump to' selector to go straight to your chosen fretboard within the document

Built for: players who like to see the relationships between notes, intervals, and shapes laid out clearly.

A document based fretboard notebook

A Frettly document is made of panels: each panel can display a given scale, scale or mode on the documents configured instrument/tuning. Show/hide toggling of notes/intervals displayed, click on notes/intervals to hear them on the chosen instrument, toggle the display of either the notes or intervals. Save different documents for different learnings, projects or tunings.

  • Multiple fretboards per document
  • Scales & chords rendered on the neck, left or right handed
  • Interval-focused view to see function, not just dots
If you’re a fellow guitar theory tinkerer, your feedback on workflows and visualisation will be especially welcome.
